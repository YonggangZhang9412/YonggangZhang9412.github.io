Biography

    My research is dedicated to developing AI systems that can be integrated into daily life in an effective, reliable manner. My research objectives are threefold: (1) Design theoretical frameworks and algorithms to evaluate and mitigate model vulnerabilities; (2) Unpack the reasoning and creative capabilities of large language models (LLMs); (3) Develop theories and algorithms to enhance the robustness of distributed learning frameworks for LLMs and AI-aided co-creation. My long-term research goal is to accelerate the seamless integration of AI systems into everyday life and drive advancements in social productivity.

    I am currently a Research Assistant Professor at Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), collaborating with Prof. Yike Guo (Fellow of IEEE and Royal Academy of Engineering). Prior to this, I served as a Postdoctoral Fellow at Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU), working with Prof. Yiu-ming Cheung (IEEE Fellow) and Prof. Bo Han. I obtained my Ph.D. degree from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in June 2022, under the supervision of Prof. Xinmei Tian.

Research Interests

    My research interests lie in Trustworthy Machine Learning, Trustworthy Machine Reasoning, Federated Learning, and Out-of-Distribution Learning.
